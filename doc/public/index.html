Learning Wellness. A variety video-series, SNL style, presenting self improvement based on the Harvard Happiness class

Explore the awesomeness

Who doesn't want to live a happier, pleasurable, more fulfilled life?

Think of a "GROWNUP Sesame Street": Conversations with spicy hosts and puppets plus "Saturday Night Live" Skits... Presenting helpful research and tools on self esteem, relationships, health, change, optimism, self-improvement, and other efficient topics.

  • Our goals

    A variety video-series targeted to global students of all ages, mostly unable to come to Harvard or afford academic education but see themselves as life-long learners with self-improvement and happiness as a goal. Based on Harvard University's most popular course "Positive Psychology" attracted 1 of every 5 grad students at Harvard.

  • Dr. Ben-Shahar course

    Noted Harvard professor Tal Ben-Shahar, who developed Harvard's successful academic program, is the force behind Learning Wellness.

While much of this extremely valuable information

has been broadly shared in academia and research papers – this presentation format makes it not widely available to the general public - until now.

  • 30 Minute Variety Series

    Explores achieving the happiest, pleasurable, most fulfilled life. The show’s unique format weaves “different elements" of commercial television: "a strong visual style, fast-moving action, humor, and music". Spots, puppets, animation, live action, and music. By using "teaching commercials", or several twelve -to ninety-second shorts, and a repetition of several key concepts throughout an episode.

  • Learning Wellness format

    Learning Wellness’s format is unique and different[af]. Our show presents experts, authors, and celebrities, exploring life topics such as economic and social success, life changes, human potential, motivation, emotional intelligence, and leadership.

Learning Wellness Proposal

Learning Wellness’s episodes are not to be missed.

Episode 1: Intro

Humanistic Psychology to Positive psychology. From asking "what is not working" to asking "what IS working” ▪ Uncovering yourself (most of the answers are inside of you not outside)

Episode 2: Positive Psychology foundations

Perspective ▪ Passive victim Vs. Active Agent ▪ Is change possible? How does Our Brain work ▪ Studying the tip of society – the study of potential ▪ Subjective well being: internal/external factors ▪ Using our nature for our best ▪ Deserving of happiness

Episode 3: Beliefs

Beliefs as self-fulfilling prophecies ▪ In the classroom ▪ Our thoughts change our immune system ▪ Resiliency (mindfulness studies of Ellen Langer) ▪ Albert Bandura on Self-Efficacy, ▪ Nathaniel Branden on Self-Esteem, taking action ▪ Imagination and the placebo effect. ▪ Cognitive therapy

Episode 4: Focus

We co-create reality ▪ The fault finder and The merit Finder ▪ A matter of interpretation "never let a good crisis go to waste" Anne Harbison. ▪ The nun-study (gratitude as a predictor of longevity) ▪ the focus of the media today ▪ Correcting the false schema, adaptation and learning to be grateful ▪ Research on gratitude

Episode 5: Change

Research on Twins ▪ Change is possible: anatomy of change, brain, fMRI(functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging) ▪ The price of quick fix ▪ Emotions: Mindful meditation ▪ Peak Experiences ▪ Taking action ▪ Behavior changing attitudes ▪ "deeds exceeding wisdom" ▪ Coping ▪ Cognition: thoughts create worlds ▪ Cognitive reconstruction (changing our perception) ▪ Journaling as an example

Episode 6: Goals

Goal Setting ▪ Therapy and Practice- goals, resilience, performance, wellbeing ▪ Being self-concordant and the benefits of being so ▪ Managing Time- work orientation and organizational psychology ▪ The three question process (meaning, pleasure, strength). ▪ Flow by Chiszentmihaly ▪ Clear sense of direction ▪ Overcoming procrastination ▪Simplifying - do less, not more ▪ The obsession with material wealth and the consequences ▪From material to happiness perception ▪ Implications on politics - Ghandi:"Be the change you want to see in the world"

Originally from Israel, Cohen came to the United States to collaborate with Professor Tal Ben Shahar, who quickly embraced Shalhavit-Simcha as his protégé. Cohen was a project director and teaching assistant in Shahar’s Positive Psychology course. Currently a Harvard graduate student in instructional design and digital media working with the MITx Entrepreneurship team on cutting edge educational video’s and MOOCs.
